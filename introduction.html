What are System and Network

Background image: Google Image

As generic as they may sound, systems and networks are key research areas in computer science whose development continues to impact our daily life. Any aspiring computer scientist should know exactly what they mean and what their respective roles are. So what are they exactly?




A computer system generally refers to a collection of software and hardware which forms a functioning computer. A system is able to receive a user’s input, process the received data, and produce a desired output . You witness a working system in action whenever you use your computer. Consider the system in play when you move your mouse; by moving the mouse, the sensor of the mouse captures your movement, the mouse then sends this input through its wire and into the Central Processing Unit (CPU) of your computer where this information is processed. The data resulting from this is then sent to your monitor, where you finally see the output of the system, being the movement of your cursor.



A network constitutes of a group of computers, servers, peripherals, or other devices connected to one another to allow the transfer and sharing of data and resources. A network of computers can be connected through cabling (such as Ethernet cables) or through wireless signals (radio waves). Networks enable different devices to interact and assist us in our daily activities beyond the capability of just one computer system. For instance, when you watch an online video, the content you are viewing cannot be found inside your computer’s memory. Instead, through a wireless network we call the Internet, your computer can receive new data and play essentially any accessible video available in the network!
